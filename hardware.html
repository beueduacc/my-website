Raspberry Pi Zero

At around the size of a thumb drive, these $5 computers are perfect for DIY projects such as cameras, speakers, smart home components, and DNS sinkholes. They also feature a standard Raspberry GPIO board and can be a great entry point for learning to code.

Raspberry Pi 5

Powered by a 64-bit quad-core Arm processor, and 800MHz VideoCore VII GPU; this is the most powerful Raspberry Pi available. With support for dual 4Kp60 HDMI displays, Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, ethernet, 2x USB 3.0, and 2x USB 2.0 the Pi 5 is capable of powering a full sized workstation.
